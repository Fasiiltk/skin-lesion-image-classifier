What is Skin Cancer?

Skin cancer is the abnormal growth of skin cells, often caused by excessive exposure to ultraviolet (UV) rays from the sun or tanning beds. It can occur anywhere on the body, but most commonly appears on sun-exposed areas such as the face, neck, and arms.

Types of Skin Cancer

  • Basal Cell Carcinoma (BCC): The most common type, usually non-lethal but requires treatment.
  • Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC): More likely to spread if untreated.
  • Melanoma: The most dangerous form that can spread rapidly.

How to Prevent Skin Cancer

  • Use sunscreen with SPF 30 or higher.
  • Avoid tanning beds and excessive sun exposure.
  • Wear protective clothing and sunglasses.
  • Examine your skin regularly for unusual changes.
  • Consult a dermatologist for routine checkups.
